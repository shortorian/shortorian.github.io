I made this conference poster in 2011 as part of an ongoing research project meant to check the Conservation of the Vector Current hypothesis (CVC), a theory in the Standard Model. CVC says, among other things, that some nuclei should decay in different ways equally often, but previous work indicated that a certain excited state of Sodium 22 might have decay modes that break CVC. I assisted in each phase of this experiment, from design to analysis, including building a beamline and operating the accelerator at the University of Washington nuclear physics lab.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

This was my second scientific poster. I’d improved a bit from the first, but there’s still a long way to go: lots of text and no clear message at first sight. I was presenting at a nuclear physics conference and it got high praise from at least one attendee, but it’s obviously extremely dense for everyone else!<\/p>\n\n\n\n
