I made the poster below as part of an internship at Berkeley National Lab in 2010. I worked with a group that did ultrafast pump-probe laser spectroscopy for materials science. In these studies, chains of extremely short laser pulses are used to “pump” a sample, changing its state, and then probe changes in the sample after excitation with the pump. These techniques are used to study exotic material properties like superconductivity. During the internship I designed an instrument called an optical parametric amplifier, which allows an operator to tune the center frequency of the ultrafast pulses in order to pump states of interest for experiments.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

This was my first research poster and largely demonstrates all the things you shouldn’t do – it has tons of text, doesn’t use figures well, and it’s mostly copied out of the final written report for the internship. Some symbols in this copy are also a bit garbled after importing a PDF to Inkscape and then exporting a PNG, but hopefully no one’s looking that closely…<\/p>\n\n\n\n
