I made this poster as part of my master’s degree in nuclear science at Simon Fraser University. I worked at TRIUMF, Canada’s National Lab for Nuclear and Particle Physics, in the TITAN group, which specializes in extremely high precision measurements of the mass of radioactive atoms. These measurements help physicists learn more about nuclear structure and also provide important constraints on astronomers’ theories about the evolution of stars. I helped develop a new spectrometer that could act as a filter for the TITAN setup, allowing access to many new nuclei by removing contaminants TRIUMF couldn’t process at the time. Our device, called a multiple reflection time of flight mass spectrometer, also allowed TRIUMF accelerator scientists to directly monitor the full contents of their radioactive ion beams (including stable components rather than just radioactive isotopes) for the first time. I report on this project in much more detail in my master’s thesis<\/a>.<\/p>\n\n\n\n
